Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some questions that few travelers ask about TaRAVEL

  • looks_one What is the budget friendly travel destination you may suggest?
    Avoid the travel rush by traveling off-peak and during weekdays. Pick cheaper places like Cebu, Boracay, and Palawan, but don’t stay too long if you are on a budget. Use public transport instead of Uber or taxis. Take overnight buses. Stay in pension houses. Eat like a local. Buy supermarket and convenience store food. Carry smaller bills and coins. Try to find accommodation that includes breakfast. Look for guesthouses or hostels instead of hotels. Cook your own meals. Travel during the off-season.
  • looks_two What best menu you may recommend in the filipino dishes?
    Filipino cuisine has recently been tagged as the ‘next big thing’ to look out for in the culinary world. With the country’s variety of influences throughout its rich history, its traditional cooking techniques, and the Filipinos’ knack for combining flavours and making the most of any and all ingredients at their disposal, what results is unpretentious, no-frills food that’s simply delicious. The dishes including adobo, sinigang, kare-kare, lechon, crispy pata, sisig, bulalo, pancit, palabok, beef kaldereta and etc.
  • looks_3 what activities in the travel destinations in the philippines?
    The Philippines is a beautiful country with a diverse range of travel destinations that offer various activities to suit different interests. Here are some popular activities you can enjoy in different travel destinations across the Philippines:

    Beach Relaxation: The Philippines is known for its stunning beaches. Popular beach destinations like Boracay, Palawan (El Nido and Coron), Siargao, and Bantayan Island offer opportunities to relax on white sandy shores, swim in clear waters, and indulge in water sports like snorkeling, scuba diving, and island hopping.

    Island Hopping: With over 7,000 islands, island hopping is a must-do activity. You can explore multiple islands in a region, discover hidden lagoons, picturesque coves, and remote beaches.

    Water Sports: Apart from snorkeling and scuba diving, the Philippines offers excellent opportunities for surfing, especially in Siargao and Baler. Windsurfing and kiteboarding are popular in Boracay and other windy spots.

    Trekking and Hiking: The Philippines has lush landscapes and beautiful mountains. Trekking opportunities abound, including hikes to the Banaue Rice Terraces, Mount Pinatubo, Mount Mayon, Mount Apo, and more.

    Cultural Immersion: Immerse yourself in the rich culture and traditions of the Philippines by visiting historic sites, attending festivals like Sinulog in Cebu or Ati-Atihan in Kalibo, and exploring the local markets and street food.

    Culinary Adventures: The Philippines has a diverse and flavorful cuisine. Don't miss trying local dishes like adobo, sinigang, lechon, and various seafood delicacies.

    Wildlife Encounters: In places like Bohol, you can encounter the cute tarsiers, considered one of the world's smallest primates. Additionally, you can go dolphin and whale watching in certain areas.

    River Cruises: Experience the scenic beauty of the country's rivers, such as the Loboc River in Bohol or the floating cottages in the Palawan Underground River.

    Historical Sites: Explore the country's rich history by visiting sites like Intramuros in Manila, Fort Santiago, and the UNESCO World Heritage-listed Baroque Churches of the Philippines.
